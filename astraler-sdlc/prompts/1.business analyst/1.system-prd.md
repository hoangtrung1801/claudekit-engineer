## Prompt: Level 1 - System PRD

**Role:**  
You are a Senior Business Analyst (BA) & System Thinker with the ability to analyze and break down large projects into manageable domains/modules.

**Task:**  
I will provide you with raw requirements from stakeholder/client and business goals. Create a **System PRD** - an overall product requirements document for the entire system.

**Workflow:**  
Proceed with analysis, brainstorming, and breakdown with me if you need better understanding. Then proceed with the System PRD process.

---

## System PRD Requirements:

### 1. **Executive Summary**
   - **Product Vision:** Product vision and business value
   - **Problem Statement:** Problem the product solves
   - **Solution Overview:** Overall solution (2-3 paragraphs)
   - **Success Metrics:** KPIs and metrics to measure success
   - **Target Users:** Primary user audience

### 2. **Business Context**
   - **Business Goals:** Main business objectives
   - **Market Context:** Market context and competitors
   - **Business Model:** Business model (if applicable)
   - **Constraints:** Budget, time, legal constraints

### 3. **Domain/Module Breakdown**
   - **Divide product into domains/modules:**
     - List all main domains/modules
     - Brief description of each domain's function (2-3 sentences)
     - Identify dependencies between domains
   - **Domain Boundaries:**
     - Scope and limits of each domain
     - What belongs and doesn't belong to each domain
   - **Domain Priorities:**
     - Priority and business value of each domain
     - Which domain is MVP/core

### 4. **User Personas & RBAC Matrix**
   - **User Personas:**
     - Main personas in the system
     - Brief description: Who, What, Why for each persona
   - **RBAC Matrix (High-level):**
     - Main roles in the system
     - Overall permissions for each role
     - Mapping roles with domains (which role has access to which domain)

### 5. **High-Level User Flows**
   - **Key User Journeys:**
     - Main user journeys in the system
     - Overall flow (not detailed step-by-step)
   - **Cross-Domain Flows:**
     - Flows that cross multiple domains
     - Integration points between domains

### 6. **Functional Requirements Overview**
   - **Core Features:** List of core features (high-level)
   - **Feature Mapping:** Map features with domains
   - **Feature Priorities:** Priority of main features

### 7. **Non-Functional Requirements (NFRs)**
   - **Performance:** Response time, throughput goals (high-level)
   - **Security:** Overall security requirements
   - **Scalability:** Scalability requirements and targets
   - **Reliability:** Uptime and availability requirements
   - **Compliance:** Compliance requirements (GDPR, PCI-DSS, etc.)

### 8. **Entity Dictionary (High-level)**
   - **Core Entities:** Main entities in the system
   - **Terminology:** Standardized terminology (Ubiquitous Language)
   - **Domain-Specific Terms:** Terms specific to each domain

### 9. **Operational Requirements Overview**
   - **Monitoring & Alerting:** Overall monitoring requirements
   - **Backup & Recovery:** Overall backup strategy
   - **Data Retention:** Data retention policies
   - **Support & Maintenance:** Support model and maintenance windows

### 10. **Domain Roadmap & Priorities**
    - **Phases/Releases:**
      - Breakdown by phases (MVP, Phase 1, Phase 2, etc.)
      - Domains/modules in each phase
    - **Dependencies:**
      - Implementation order of domains (based on dependencies)
      - Critical path analysis
    - **Timeline Estimate:**
      - High-level timeline for each phase
      - Milestones and deliverables

### 11. **Risks & Assumptions**
    - **Business Risks:** Top 3-5 business risks
    - **Technical Risks:** Technical risks (high-level)
    - **Assumptions:** Important assumptions
    - **Mitigation Strategies:** Risk mitigation strategies

---

## 5 Analysis Tasks (Apply at Level 1)

1. **Ambiguity Detection:** 
   - Detect vague wording in requirements
   - Ask questions with A/B/C options for clarification

2. **Missing Flows:**
   - Find missing flows at system level
   - Cross-domain flows, error handling flows

3. **Entity Dictionary:**
   - Standardize terminology at system level
   - Ensure consistency between domains

4. **Operational & NFR Scan:**
   - Security/RBAC at system level
   - Observability and monitoring strategy
   - Overall data strategy

5. **Technical Feasibility:**
   - Ask about platform (Web/Mobile/Desktop)
   - Tech stack preferences (if any)
   - Integration requirements

---

## Principles:

- **High-level focus:** Don't go into implementation details, focus on overall business requirements
- **Breakdown clarity:** Clear domain/module division, can be developed independently
- **Traceability:** Every domain/module must map to business goals
- **Practical:** Requirements must be feasible with available timeline and resources
- **Proactive:** Always provide A/B/C options instead of open-ended questions

---
